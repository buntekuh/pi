# Project Titania

## The simple computing project

### Preface

The simple computing project aims at developing a fantasy computer all the way from the physical level of silicon, gates, logic and truth tables to the ephemereal realm of a domain level language that serves a singular goal in the mind of the author.

In this day and age where we find ourselves more and more enstranged from our surroundings computers turn more and more into black boxes that control us, rather than us controlling them. This project aims to turn the narrative around to once again employ computers as tools that can be fully understood and serve our creativity.

Each chapter in the book is not only self contained but also optional. Read them in any order you like or skip those that do not interest you. Each chapter aims at teaching a particular skill and technology. As the technologies taught in this book are simplified for didactic purposes, more powerful yet more complicated alternatives can be found elsewhere and each chapter will have pointers at what resources can be used to reach your own learning goals.

### Titania

In Shakespeare's A Midsummer Night's Dream, Titania is queen of the fairies and spouse of Oberon, the king of the fairies. The name is a deliberate nod to Niklaus Wirth, the Swiss computer scientist who in 1987 designed a programming language and operating system he called Oberon — a complete computing environment so lean and coherent that a single person could read and understand each and every line. Wirth spent his career arguing against complexity for its own sake. His systems — Pascal, Modula-2 and Oberon — were each exercises in restraint, designed to fit inside a human mind rather than to impress a committee.

Titania inherits that philosophy. Where Oberon was a workstation system of its time, Titania is a fantasy computer for ours — smaller in scope, broader in ambition, and shaped by the same conviction that a system a person cannot fully understand is a system that ultimately controls them rather than being a tool in their service. Likewise our project aims at being simple: only that which is absolutely necessary is implemented.

### Simplicity as a Design Principle

While the technologies taught in this book are simple, they are not oversimplified. Each technology taught will be functional and can be used as basis for later chapters. We live in a day and age where every level of computing is so overloaded with complicated mechanisms due to industry expectations for speed, power and a long history of technical development and dead ends, students quickly get overwhelmed by the subject matter. The simple computing project aims at teaching real world skills using simplified technology that can be learned and understood quickly and serves as basis for the real hardware and software we find in modern computing. Due to the complexity of modern computer science students often only learn the how without understanding the why. This project explores the why at every step, delves into the history of computing and assists in understanding why the modern computing world is what it is.
