# Biro Factory — Plot Summary

## Characters

- **Crumpet Wagsworth** — the player. Youngest son, cheerful, oblivious, surprisingly effective.
- **Lord Wagsworth** — patriarch. Menacing eyebrow. Delivers the sentence.
- **Aunt Mildred** — the villain. Cold. Running a black market ammunition operation through the factory.
- **Aunt Edwina** — rasping, disapproving.
- **Uncle Basil** — ex-regiment. Arrives at 6pm to inspect. Furious about the statuary.
- **Bertie** — friend. Knows black market ammunition prices. Source of the last line.
- **Fotherby** — friend. Golfer. Has a contact at the Home Office. Stops texting and calls.
- **Miriam** — friend. Texts about tennis.
- **The previous director** — resigned the morning of Act 1. Pushed out by Mildred.


## Act 1 — A Stain on the Family Escutcheon

The assembled family berates Crumpet for his latest transgression. One by one they deliver
their verdict. The player cannot move or speak. Lord Wagsworth, as the storm subsides, muses
that the director of the biro factory resigned this morning — a simple post, even for a cretin,
the factory practically runs itself. Crumpet is dispatched. He is teleported to the factory lobby.


## Act 2 — The Biro Factory

### Arrival

The smartphone buzzes immediately: Miriam has a court booked, Fotherby says the course is in
magnificent condition. The purple robot, wearing an apron, is dusting the bookshelves. The lobby
has a cardboard box containing a red herring. At one spot on the factory floor, cartridges are
piling up at an empty station — robots faithfully delivering to somewhere a machine used to be.

### Mildred's Visit

Mildred sweeps in. Uncle Basil will inspect at 6pm. She dismissed the staff. The factory is in
disarray. Good luck with that. She storms out. This is the deadline.

### The Factory

The robots are not scrambled. They are running the ammunition production program faithfully.
But Mildred removed the cleaning machine and hid it in a closet, jamming the line. Cartridges
pile up at the empty station. Nothing moves forward.

Rooms:
- **Lobby** — two robots, command cubes scattered, cardboard box, pile of cartridges at empty station
- **Red Room** — shell manufacturing machine (jammed: red herring lodged inside)
- **Green Room** — filling station (ink supply locked)
- **Purple Room** — assembly machine
- **QA Room** — gun, fifty-foot shooting range (locked initially)
- **Foreman's office** — logbook showing correct factory layout (locked)
- **Closet** — the cleaning machine, hidden here by Mildred

### Puzzles

1. **The routing puzzle** — reroute robots to deliver cartridges to the filling station instead
   of the empty station. The foreman's logbook shows the correct biro configuration.

2. **The jammed shell machine** — the red herring from the lobby box is lodged inside. A robot
   with a broken program has been carrying it around the factory.

3. **The locked ink store** — key is in the foreman's apron, hanging on the purple robot.

4. **The cart puzzle** — find a cart, harness two robots to it, retrieve the cleaning machine
   from the closet, return it to the empty station. It installs. It strips ink from cartridges.
   In a factory that assembles new cartridges, this is a dead end. Move on.

5. **The QA room** — fire a biro from the production line. Blue ink splotch on the target.
   You bring the nib very close to your eye. Something inside glints in a way that ink does not
   glint. Was the shell meant to contain ink at all?

6. **The phone investigation** — text Fotherby about the compound stabiliser caskets sitting
   uselessly in the factory. Text about the cleaning machine. The picture assembles across
   several turns. Fotherby stops texting and calls.

### The Revelation

The factory has two modes:

- **Biro mode** — shell → fill cartridge with ink → assemble
- **Ammunition mode** — shell → clean cartridge (remove ink) → fill with compound stabiliser → assemble

The cleaning machine's only purpose is to strip ink before the stabiliser goes in. The caskets
of compound stabiliser serve no purpose in biro production. They were feedstock for ammunition
mode. The pile of cartridges at the empty station is the line jammed mid-crime.

Mildred moved the cleaning machine to hide the evidence. She assumed Crumpet would never notice
the empty station, never find the closet, never manage to move the machine back even if he did.
She dismissed the staff so there were no witnesses. She arranged the previous director's departure.
She sent the family's most reliably useless member to deal with it.

### The Factory Runs

Once the biro configuration is running, biros come off the line. Crumpet feels enormously pleased
with himself. He finds the QA room gun. He goes outside.


## Act 3 — The Curtain

Crumpet is on the lawn, taking pot shots at the busts of Roman emperors, when Basil and Mildred
arrive. Basil is furious about the statuary. Mildred sees the gun and blanches. Crumpet looks
at her face and in one moment of social clarity — the one thing he has always been able to read —
understands.

**"I say, Auntie, Bertie tells me explosive shells sell awfully well on the black market."**

Basil stops being angry about Hadrian.

Curtain.


## Notes

- Leaving the factory at any point triggers the bad ending: steamboat to South Africa.
- The smartphone is both temptation (golf, tennis) and tool (the investigation).
- The compound stabiliser caskets are the physical evidence. Their presence in a biro factory
  is the crime.
- Crumpet never understands what he has uncovered. He understands only that Mildred is guilty.
  That is enough.
